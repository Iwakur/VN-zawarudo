# VN-zawarudo

Short concept guide for the visual novel.

## Core Idea

`VN-zawarudo` is a post-apocalyptic political/sci-fi visual novel set in a rebuilt but unstable Europe after nuclear destruction. The story mixes ruined history, new kingdoms, nomadic states, experimental technology, and strange particle-based phenomena called `ferons`.

The central conflict starts around a suspicious concentration of ferons in Central Europe. Different rulers understand that something dangerous is forming, but nobody fully knows whether it is a weapon, a new technology, or the beginning of another catastrophe.

## Story Premise

The opening plot has two connected sides:

- **Gleb in Brussels**: a ruler/scientist figure who has rebuilt Brussels into a clean, controlled, almost utopian capital. Under the surface, he runs a secret underground project involving artificial bodies, bio-engineered workers, white steel, and the creation of `Lane`, an "ideal human" made from pure white metal.
- **Artem and Maksym**: rulers/allies meeting in a nomadic Buryat-style camp near the Southern Bug. Their scene is more human, informal, and humorous, but it reveals the bigger geopolitical danger: ferons are gathering in Europe, and Gleb's closed territory may be connected.

The novel should feel like a world where old Europe died, but its borders, myths, grudges, and ambitions returned in distorted forms.

## World Style

The map suggests an alternate Europe divided between new regimes, kingdoms, tribal/confederate lands, and collapsed zones. The setting is not clean fantasy and not pure cyberpunk. It is closer to:

- post-nuclear Europe
- neo-medieval politics
- dieselpunk / retrofuturist machinery
- bioengineering horror
- folk and steppe influences
- dark political satire

Important visual contrast:

- **Gleb's world**: clean white rooms, palace gardens, rebuilt Brussels, secret labs, steel prosthetics, glass capsules, cables, red alarm lights.
- **Artem and Maksym's world**: burned steppe, horses, yurts, flags, tea, smoke, rough travel clothing, nomadic luxury, warmer colors.
- **The wider world**: damaged maps, broken borders, improvised states, old-world ruins, military paranoia.

## Tone

The tone should move between comedy and horror. Characters speak casually, sometimes absurdly or aggressively, but the world behind them is brutal. This contrast is important: jokes and vulgar dialogue make the rulers feel alive, while the background reminds the player that these people control armies, experiments, and ruined nations.

Recommended tone keywords:

- darkly funny
- political
- post-apocalyptic
- violent when needed
- surreal sci-fi
- informal but serious underneath

## Main Characters / Factions

- **Gleb**: ruler of rebuilt Brussels and leader of a secret scientific project. Cold, proud, theatrical, but emotionally attached to his creation, Lane.
- **Lane**: artificial "ideal human" made from white steel. Currently more of a symbolic center than an active character.
- **Filip**: older political partner/advisor around Gleb. Represents old-world diplomacy and uneasy alliances.
- **Artem**: King of Britain. Travels secretly, jokes often, but carries guilt from past violence, especially the burning of Saxon London.
- **Maksym / Maksymilian-khan**: nomadic ruler connected to Buryat culture and shaman-like feron abilities. Warns Artem about the European anomaly.
- **Owens brothers / guards**: companions used for action, comedy, and outsider perspective.

## Visual Direction

Use strong contrast between locations:

- **Brussels / labs**: white, black, steel, pale yellow liquid, sterile interiors, sharp shadows, red emergency lighting.
- **Steppe / camp**: dry grass, dust, horses, felt yurts, colorful flags, firelight, tea, smoke, worn fabric.
- **Memory / nightmare scenes**: red sky, black ash, burning streets, silhouettes, heavy smoke, high contrast.
- **Maps and UI**: hand-drawn tactical notes, rough borders, faction markings, notebook/sketchbook feeling.

The best overall style would be gritty anime-inspired visual novel art with political map overlays and occasional horror imagery.
